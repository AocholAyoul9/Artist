About the Artist

Joseph Kangi, born in 1983 in Aweil, Northern Bahr el Ghazal State, South Sudan, is a distinguished visual artist whose work bridges cultural memory and contemporary expression. His art reflects a deep-rooted connection to his heritage, brought to life through the tactile warmth of clay, the richness of ebony wood, and an evocative use of color.

Through sculpture and form, Kangi preserves the stories, values, and aesthetics of his people—transforming memory into tangible legacy.

Artistic Journey

Upbringing and Formation: A Journey in Search of Beauty

Kangi's early life took him across several Sudanese cities—including Al-Fashir, Wau, and Malakal—each leaving an imprint on his artistic worldview. In 2009, he graduated from the Sculpture Department at Sudan University of Science and Technology, combining academic knowledge with personal vision.

A Rich Artistic Career: Between Clay, Colors, and Memory

Kangi’s career began as a teaching assistant at the University of Juba. From there, he expanded globally, with his work featured in international exhibitions:

  • 2023: Zerfes Forum – New Jersey, USA
  • 2021: Group Exhibition – Oslo, Norway
  • 2020: Global Auction – London, UK
  • 2019: U.S. Embassy Exhibition – Juba
  • 2018: Zandooka Festival – Kampala, Uganda

He taught art to refugees on the Greek island of Lesbos and established a studio there. He also studied graphic design in New Delhi and worked in antiquities restoration at the National Museum in Khartoum.

Philosophy

“Art is the voice of a nation and the soul of its people. I shape memory with my hands—through clay and wood—to ensure that the stories of our ancestors live on.”
– Joseph Kangi

Kangi views art as a universal language. Through his creations, he strives to beautify cities, preserve collective memory, and inspire cultural pride across generations.

Specialties:

  • Sculpture in Clay and Ebony Wood
  • Ethnographic and Cultural Representation in Visual Art
  • Restoration of Antiquities and Historical Artifacts
  • Charcoal Drawing
